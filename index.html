THE JOURNEY
A PHYSICIST'S TRAJECTORY

From curiosity
to quantum radar

Yusuf Berat Ateş — physics undergraduate at METU, optics & photonics. Scroll to follow the path, one question leading to the next.

BEGIN↓
01 — ORIGIN · the spark of life

What separates the living from dead matter?

Nature documentaries first ignited my curiosity — watching animals hunt, grieve, and form societies made me ask what divides living things from inert matter.

In high school, biology led me to the cell, then to DNA — where chemistry revealed atoms and molecules quietly obeying precise rules.

02 — THE RULES · into physics

The rules pointed to the deepest place I could find.

Atoms and molecules, unconsciously obeying exact laws, guided me toward the broadest framework there is — physics.

In my final year of high school I worked through MIT OpenCourseWare on Quantum Mechanics, and knew I would stay in physics for good.

03 — THE SEARCH · reading alone

No one could guide me — so I read everything.

Fascinated by light and entanglement, I began hunting the blind spots of classical radar, where stealth targets and noise defeat detection. The answer kept pointing to the quantum.

My professors had no one to mentor this; they told me to read PhD-level work. So I did — five-plus papers and Marco Lanzagorta's Quantum Radar — through an earthquake, a surgery, and a broken leg. The drive never broke.

04 — THE LAB · theory meets the bench

Seeing the equations in real light.

METU gave me both theory and hands. In the optics and modern-physics labs I aligned interferometers, resolved spectra, and measured the photon–matter interaction myself.

Quantum Mechanics Elementary Solid State Laser Applications Optics & Waves Lab

05 — EXPERIMENT · applied steps

Turning the reading into code & measurement.

Now the work goes hands-on: simulations of illumination schemes in Python and MATLAB, optical-design models in Zemax, and the modern-physics experiments I'm running this term.

Electron diffraction, Franck–Hertz, photoelectric work functions, Compton scattering — quantum behavior, measured by hand.

06 — QUANTUM DEVICES · the goal

Building the source of entangled light.

The destination: quantum devices for radar and sensing — entangled signal–idler photon pairs from a nonlinear crystal, the heart of quantum illumination.

This is where theory becomes hardware — the photonics that could let radar see what classical systems cannot.

07 — THE FIELD · into the world

From the bench to systems that protect the world.

The endpoint isn't a paper — it's deployed quantum radar and sensing. I want to contribute to academia, then carry those findings into R&D, where they detect what cannot be seen and help keep people safe.
